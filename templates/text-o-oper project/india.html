Text Summarization

Brief and Perform

Extractive Summarization:

Extracts key sentences or phrases directly from the original text.
Identifies important information based on criteria such as sentence importance, relevance, and coherence.
Utilizes techniques like ranking sentences using algorithms and extracting the top-ranked ones.

Abstractive Summarization:

Generates a summary by paraphrasing and rephrasing the original content.
Involves understanding the meaning of the text and expressing it in a concise manner using new words and structures.
Requires natural language understanding and often employs deep learning models, like transformers.

Content Preservation:

Ensuring that the summary retains the most critical information from the original text.

HandwritingRecognition

Brief

Definition:

Handwriting recognition is the automated process of converting handwritten text into digital text, enabling computers to interpret and understand written input.

Process: 80%

Involves the use of image processing techniques to analyze and interpret handwritten characters or words.
Typically includes segmentation, feature extraction, and classification steps to identify individual characters.

Techniques: 60%

It uses Feature Extraction and machine learning models like deep learning methods including Convolutionary neural network and Recurrent neural network.

Text Translation

Brief and Perform

Definition:

Text translation is the process of converting written text from a source language into an equivalent text in a target language while preserving the meaning.

Machine Translation(MT):

Rule-Based MT: Early approach using linguistic rules and dictionaries for translation.
Statistical MT: Leveraged statistical models trained on bilingual corpora to predict translations.
Neural MT: Current dominant paradigm, employing neural networks, particularly sequence-to-sequence models, for more context-aware and fluent translations.


Languages and Domains:

Translation systems support various language pairs and may be specialized for specific domains like legal, medical, technical, or general conversation.
